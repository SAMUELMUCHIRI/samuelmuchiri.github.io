Project Overview

Nairobi’s traffic problem is no longer just about delays. It’s a safety issue, an accessibility issue, and a quality-of-life issue rolled into one. Busy urban corridors like Moi Avenue highlight this perfectly. Heavy vehicle traffic mixes with large numbers of pedestrians, public transport stops spill into walking space, and traffic signals remain static regardless of real-time conditions. The result is inefficient flow, frequent near-misses, and constant frustration for everyone on the road.

Most of the traffic management systems in use today were designed for far simpler conditions. They rely on fixed timing plans and limited sensing, which means they struggle to adapt to sudden changes like crowd surges, accidents, public events, or weather disruptions. They also don’t account well for vulnerable road users, including pedestrians, cyclists, and people with disabilities, whose safety and mobility needs are not always prioritized.

This project explores how Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) technology can offer a smarter alternative. By enabling real-time communication between vehicles, traffic signals, infrastructure, and pedestrians, V2X creates the foundation for a truly adaptive traffic management system. Instead of static signal cycles, intersections can respond dynamically to actual demand. Pedestrian crossings can become safer and more visible within the traffic flow. Emergency vehicles can receive priority routing. Congestion can be detected and eased before it becomes gridlock.

Using Moi Avenue as a real-world test corridor, the project focuses on designing and prototyping a V2X-based traffic management solution tailored to Nairobi’s unique mobility challenges. The goal is not just smoother traffic, but safer streets, better accessibility, and a system that works for all road users. Ultimately, this project aims to demonstrate how locally adapted smart traffic technology can help Kenyan cities move closer to safer, more efficient, and more inclusive urban mobility.

Key Features

Reliability

The system was designed to operate in real time with minimal latency. Traffic data from vehicles, pedestrian sensors, and intersections was processed instantly, allowing signals and alerts to respond within milliseconds. This ensured timely decision-making at crossings and junctions, prevented delays in safety warnings, and kept traffic flow adjustments accurate even during peak congestion.

Telemetrics & Alerts

The system continuously collected telemetry data from vehicles, roadside units, and pedestrian devices, including speed, position, and congestion levels. This data powered real-time alerts for potential hazards such as crowded crossings, sudden slowdowns, or emergency vehicle approach. Drivers and pedestrians received timely notifications through the mobile app and roadside displays, helping them react early and avoid dangerous situations.

Performance Optimization

System performance was driven by advanced algorithms that analyzed live traffic patterns to optimize signal timing, route prioritization, and pedestrian crossing windows. These algorithms adapted continuously to changing conditions, reducing congestion hotspots, minimizing wait times, and ensuring smooth coordination between vehicles and pedestrians without sacrificing safety.

Easy Integration

The solution was built to integrate smoothly with existing traffic infrastructure, including traffic lights, and road sensors already in place. Using standard communication protocols and modular hardware, the system could be added without major changes to current setups, enabling cities to upgrade to smart traffic management gradually and cost-effectively.
